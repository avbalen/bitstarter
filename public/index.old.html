According to a recent survey [1], what senior citizens fear most is the loss of independence. Living in your own home, in your own neighborhood or environment is very important for seniors. In the old days, a lot of people used to live together in extended families, families of three or more generations. In those families the young generation more or less naturally took care for the old and the grandparents happily supervised their grandchildern. In the modern age, families are scattered over cities, states and even continents so this mutual care is hardly seen. Family ties remain strong in most cases so the question "Is Granny Safe?" (and you can substitute Granny by the family member, friend or relative you care about) is still a valid question.

How it works

The service "Is Granny Safe?" (www.isgrannysafe.com) uses the sensors of a modern smartphone to keep track of the movements and location of its owner. The smartphone sends periodically the sensor data to a central system. This system gradually learns what the normal pattern of movements for the smartphone owner is so that after a while the system can generate an alert when an unusual pattern of movements (or no movement at all) is found. The alert is first sent to the smartphone to check if there was really something strange going on. If the smartphone owner is not responding, the alert will be forwarded to someone who is able to assist.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the survey you are referring to as [1]?
"Aging in Place in America" prepared by Prince Market Research, August 20, 2007
How is the privacy of the members guaranteed?
Each member of "Is Granny Safe?" has an own unique userid and password. Members cannot see each others data. Moreover, all data is stored encrypted on the servers.
So the owner should carry the smartphone at all times?
Yes, because only in that way sudden events like a fall or collapse can be detected. Putting the smartphone down for a nap is OK as the system is soon learning what your usual nap-times are so you will not be disturbed.
