According to a recent survey [1], what senior citizens fear most is the loss of independence. Living in your own home, in your own neighborhood or environment is very important for seniors. In the old days, a lot of people used to live together in extended families, families of three or more generations. In those families the young generation more or less naturally took care for the old and the grandparents happily supervised their grandchildern. In the modern age, families are scattered over cities, states and even continents so this mutual care is hardly seen. Family ties remain strong in most cases so the question "Is Granny Safe?" (and you can substitute Granny by the family member, friend or relative you care about) is still a valid question.

How it works

The service "Is Granny Safe?" (www.isgrannysafe.com) uses the sensors of a modern smartphone to keep track of the movements and location of its owner. The smartphone sends periodically the sensor data to a central system. This system gradually learns what the normal pattern of movements (and locations) for the smartphone owner is so that after a while the system can generate an alert when an unusual pattern of movements (or no movement at all) is found. The alert is first sent to the smartphone to check if there was really something unusual happening. If the smartphone owner is not responding, the alert will be forwarded to someone who is able to assist. When needed, other people can be signalled at the same time.

Why you (and Granny!) should care

Knowing that the people you love most are taken care of is very important. For Granny, to know that even when an accident happens or sudden illness strikes she is not alone, someone is coming for assistance is very reassuring. For you, to know that even if you live at a distance, Granny is being assisted whenever she needs help can possibly make life a little bit easier for you.

But remember... Granny still loves to hear your voice! Don't forget to call her often and don't forget her birthday!

Frequently Asked Questions

Which survey are you referring to as [1]?
"Aging in Place in America" prepared by Prince Market Research: August 20, 2007
What about security?
Each member of "Is Granny Safe?" has an own account and the smartphone is associated uniquely to the account. All data on the servers is strongly encrypted.
So the owner should carry the smartphone at all times?
Yes, because only in that way sudden events like a fall or collapse can be detected. Putting the smartphone down for a nap is OK as the system is soon learning what your usual nap-times are so you are still being monitored.
How about the privacy of the person carrying the smartphone?
Most people are quick in realizing that the benefits of having nearly immediate assistance in an emergency soon overcomes the slight reduction in privacy as a result of monitoring all movements. As the sensor data is analyzed directly when it arrives at the servers, no privacy-sensitive data is ever recorded.
